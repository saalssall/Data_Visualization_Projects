Project 01

Gun Deaths in the US (2012–2014)

Dataset — death_data.csv
Tools — Python, Pandas, NumPy
Type — Exploratory Analysis

An exploratory analysis of gun-related deaths in the United States between 2012 and 2014. The dataset was examined across multiple dimensions including gender, race, and cause of death to identify patterns and distributions within the data.

Key Findings
Gender
Males are significantly more likely to die by gun suicide than females, a pattern that remained consistent across all three years.
Race
White Americans accounted for approximately 66% of gun deaths per 100,000 people — the highest of any racial group. Native Americans had the lowest rate at around 0.9%.
Cause of death
Suicide was the most common cause of gun deaths, accounting for the majority of incidents across all three years.
Annual trend
The percentage of gun deaths per 100,000 people showed notable change between 2012 and 2014, indicating a shifting trend over this period.
